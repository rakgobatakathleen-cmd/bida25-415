Explore Our Destinations

Unveil the majestic landscapes and untold stories of Africa's premier wildlife haven.

Okavango Delta

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Okavango Delta is an inland river delta known for its sprawling grassy plains, which flood seasonally, creating a lush animal habitat. It's a sanctuary for a myriad of wildlife, from big cats to endangered wild dogs.

Activities & Highlights

  • Mokoro (dug-out canoe) excursions
  • Guided walking safaris on delta islands
  • Exceptional bird watching
  • Scenic helicopter flights

Chobe National Park

Renowned for having the largest concentration of elephants in Africa, Chobe is arguably Botswana’s most accessible big game safari destination. The life-giving Chobe River attracts vast herds of buffalo and antelope, pursued inevitably by apex predators.

Activities & Highlights

  • Afternoon river cruises
  • Open 4x4 game drives
  • Photography boat safaris
  • Visit local communities in Kasane

Kalahari Desert & Makgadikgadi Pans

Experience true isolation in the stunning expanse of the Kalahari Desert. The adjacent Makgadikgadi Pans, remnants of a massive ancient lake, transform into a vibrant wetland after rains, drawing one of Africa’s largest zebra migrations.

Activities & Highlights

  • Interactions with the indigenous San people
  • Quad biking across the alien landscape of the pans
  • Encountering habituated meerkats
  • Searching for the elusive black-maned Kalahari lions

Essential Travel Advice

Security

Botswana is considered one of the safest countries in Africa for travelers. Political stability and a low crime rate mean you can focus entirely on your adventure. Always follow your guide's advice when in the presence of wildlife.

Health Requirements

Some regions, particularly the northern wet areas (Delta and Chobe), are malaria zones. Consult your doctor regarding anti-malarial medication before travel. High-quality private medical facilities are available in Gaborone and Maun.

Travel Logistics

The primary gateway is Maun International Airport. From there, transfers to camps are typically via light aircraft—a scenic experience itself! Pack light, in soft-sided bags, as luggage allowances on bush flights are strictly enforced.
